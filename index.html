By Facundo Leguizamón Pfeffer & Oscar Möller

Automated Determination of the Strength of Arbitrary Sections of Structural Concrete

A free, easy to use software that generates three-dimensional interaction diagrams of combined axial-force/biaxial-bending for structural concrete sections using the Python coding language and Excel VBA. In accordance with CIRSOC 201 building code standards, the software allows users to define custom sections with any given geometry, type of reinforcement (prestressed included), and placement.

A bit of
context...

The main objective in the Structural Engineering field is to design structures which are capable of resisting loads (i.e. natural or anthropic forces), transmitting them to a foundation in an efficient, stable and safe way, during its intended life period. When designing structural members that are part of buildings, roads and bridges, the most common internal forces that should be resisted by these members are Axial Force and Bending Moment

A solution
for civil engineers

The determination of the strength of structural concrete members (reinforced or prestressed) subjected to Bending Moments and Axial Forces, requires resolving non-linear differential equations which makes the process considerably complex.
As a consequence, Biaxial Bending – Axial Force Interaction Diagrams have been extensively used as a practical tool in the industry. In these diagrams, the axes represent the mentioned forces, and a contour of safe M, N combinations is depicted.
Interaction diagrams limit themselves to particularly conventional cases (simple and rectangular geometries, symmetric distribution of reinforcements, etc.), which is useful but does not allow for optimal designs or exploring the full potential of the materials used.

OUR MISSION

This Research work aims to reverse this situation by developing an automated computer system that allows the assessment of strength for concrete elements with any geometry and type of reinforcement. This system provides users with complete freedom to explore and exhaust possibilities through the generation of Interaction Diagrams.
